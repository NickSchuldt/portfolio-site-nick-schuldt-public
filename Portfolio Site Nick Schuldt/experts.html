Overview- Experts a UX Case Study

  • Challenge: Every day people encounter problems in their daily life. Most of the time they are able to solve it themselves. However; some tasks can be just a little out of their wheel house. This is where Experts can help users by connecting them with a consultant to help walk them through their problem.
  • Solution: Experts is a smartphone app that provides users the ability to video call consultants that have expert knowledge in order to help our users solve problems that may be to difficult to solve on their own.
  • My Role: This project was created as part of my UX/UI designer certification. My role in this project involved: User Research, creating user personas/journeys, user flows, wireframing, prototyping and user testing.
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Balsamiq, Optimal Sort, Usability Hub
  • Duration: July 2020-March 2021

Problem Statement

Our users need an app that allows them to connect to experts for virtually any questions they may have.  We need to make sure this is done in a timely manner with a great deal of accuracy while also making the user feel like they are getting the most out of there money.  Along with this we need to make sure they can find an expert in the field they want with ease. We will know this to be true when a user can find the expert they need in minutes along with getting a accurate response/reply within a couple hours of posting there question and ultimately coming back for futures questions that may require an expert.

Surveys

A short online survey was conducted in order to gain insight and guidance on desirability for this service, pricing, features, and current competition.

  • The 3 features that received overwhelming support were implemented. Features that received less than 50% support were lowered in priority or removed from the list completely.
  • 15 response
  • 14 questions
  • 100% Wanted an Appointment Feature
  • 87% Wanted a Messenger Feature

Interviews

User Interviews were conducted in order to dive deeper into users feelings about the app. Along with having users elaborate more on some of their responses in order to gain better insight as to why they answered a certain way.

  • This allowed us to dig deeper into answers in order to gather more information than the survey could provide us.
  • There were 18 total open ended questions in order to give freedom to the interviewee and not to narrow down their responses.
  • 3 users were interviewed during this process.
  • The biggest questions looking to be answered were what features would they like to get out of this app and what price they would be willing to pay for.
  • Affinity maps were made for all 3 participants and then combined into a single affinity map

Personas

Based off of the user interviews 4 personas were created (2 based off of users in need of help and 2 being consultants) for reference throughout every stage of the design process

  • User research was done in the form of user interviews in order to build these 4 personas
  • Each persona provided demographic information, needs and goals, problems, their everyday activities, and their technology usage.
  • The personas helped dictate the order screens would be presented, along with features. Some features added for the personas was a Calendar, a message all buttons, a sub category screen and several other features.
  • The personas were reflected on before and after wireframes were made, before and after a mid-fidelity prototype, and before during, and after each iteration of the high fidelity prototype created.

User Jouney & User Flows

User journeys were then made for all 4 of the user personas in order to best achieve their most important goals.

  • User journey maps were created in order to help us better understand goals and expectations my users may experience. Along with this it helped define tasks the app would need to perform for different users.
  • By creating these user journeys it helped me realize certain features I didn't previously think of. 1 such feature would be a message all for someone who is in a rush and wanted immediate help. Another issue that was raised was making sure people knew who were online and offline.
  • All of the user journey's for my personas were created from the data received from the survey and user interviews.
  • After creating user journey's user flows were then created in order to seem them achieve a success criterion

Card Sorting & Site Mapping

Card sorting was done in order to test out the first iteration of my site map.

  • I wanted to see how and if users would group categories into the predetermined locations
  • This was conducted online using Optimal Sort
  • Along with the card sort, their was a quick 3 question open ended survey to try and gather more information on potential improvements.
  • Calendar and Payment Methods were removed from the Profile Information section and given their own section.
  • Along with this I changed "Experts" to "Consultants" since their appeared to be confusion.

Useability Testing

Use ability testing was done in order to find any areas of friction for my users. This would help me create a better easier to use product for the final iteration.

  • My goal for this round of user testing was to find any critical errors in my design. Other secondary goals were to find any minor issues with the design that could be fixed immediately or at a later date depending on the severity.
  • This was done once the mid-fidelity prototype was created.
  • The 6 participants were all between 19-27 years of age. This met my business requirements document. However; it should be noted it does leave out the higher age range (40 years old) of my BRD.
  • All 6 of these were moderated remote tests that were recorded to look back at in the future.
  • After this I found a critical error of no billing address in my design. I also found several other minor friction points that were recorded in my rainbow spread sheet, along with the critical ones.

UI Design

After testing the mid-fidelity prototype I moved on to creating the high-fidelity mock up.

  • The styling of the app was meant to feel like a corporate style.
  • This style was chosen to give users a more official feeling in order to give credibility to the app
  • The design was built to be used on smartphones
